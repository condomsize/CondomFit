Complete physical model: pressure exerted by a condom on an erect penis — case of the axially blocked membrane (λ_z = 1).

Go to Calculator → 📊 Context & statistics

1 · Model Hypotheses

  • Geometry: the penis is modelled as a rigid cylinder of radius R = C/(2π), where C is the erect circumference.
  • Thin membrane: the condom is a thin cylindrical membrane of initial thickness t₀ ≪ R. Laplace's law applies.
  • Material: natural latex, modelled as an incompressible neo-Hookean solid. Strain energy: W = C₁(I₁ − 3), with C₁ = E/6 (exact for ν = 0.5).
  • Homogeneous deformation: stretches are uniform over the entire surface.
  • Axial condition: the condom is axially blocked — its length does not change, so λ_z = 1. An axial stress σ_z > 0 arises as a consequence.
  • Radial condition: the outer surface of the condom is free (no external contact), so σ_r = 0.

2 · Step-by-Step Derivation

1

Principal Stretches

The condom (initial circumference C₀ = 2w) is stretched over the penis (circumference C). We define:

λ_θ = C / C₀ = C / (2w)  [circumferential stretch, > 1]
λ_z = L / L₀  [axial stretch, to be determined]
λ_r = t / t₀  [radial stretch = thinning]

Incompressibility: λ_r · λ_θ · λ_z = 1, hence:

λ_r = 1 / (λ_θ · λ_z)
2

Neo-Hookean model — Cauchy stress tensor

For an incompressible neo-Hookean material, the Cauchy stress in principal direction i is:

σ_i = 2C₁ λ_i² − p

where p is a Lagrange multiplier (hydrostatic pressure). Explicitly:

σ_r = 2C₁ λ_r² − p = 2C₁ / (λ_θ² λ_z²) − p
σ_θ = 2C₁ λ_θ² − p
σ_z = 2C₁ λ_z² − p
3

Radial boundary condition: σ_r = 0

The outer surface is free, so σ_r = 0:

0 = 2C₁ / (λ_θ² λ_z²) − p ⟹ p = 2C₁ / (λ_θ² λ_z²)
4

Axial boundary condition: λ_z = 1

The condom is axially blocked — its length is fixed, so:

λ_z = 1 (imposed)

The axial stress is then non-zero (it is the reaction to the constraint):

σ_z = 2C₁ (λ_z² − 1/(λ_θ² λ_z²)) = 2C₁ (1 − 1/λ_θ²) = 2C₁(1 − λ_θ−2) > 0
5

Radial stretch λ_r and current thickness

By incompressibility with λ_z = 1:

λ_r = 1 / (λ_θ · λ_z) = 1 / (λ_θ · 1) = λ_θ−1

Incompressibility check:

λ_r · λ_θ · λ_z = λ_θ−1 · λ_θ · 1 = λ_θ0 = 1 ✓

The current thickness is therefore:

t = t₀ · λ_r = t₀ / λ_θ
6

Circumferential stress σ_θ

Substituting p and λ_z = λ_θ−½ :

σ_θ = 2C₁ λ_θ² − 2C₁ / (λ_θ² · λ_z²) = 2C₁ λ_θ² − 2C₁ / (λ_θ² · 1)
σ_θ = 2C₁ (λ_θ² − λ_θ−2)
7

Membrane tension T

T = σ_θ · t = 2C₁ (λ_θ² − λ_θ−2) · t₀/λ_θ
T = 2C₁ t₀ (λ_θ − λ_θ−3)
8

Pressure via Laplace's Law — cylinder

For a thin cylindrical membrane, Laplace's law gives ΔP = T/R, with R = C/(2π):

ΔP = T / R = [2C₁ t₀ / R] · (λ_θ − λ_θ−3)

with C₁ = E/6, λ_θ = C/(2w), R = C/(2π)

5 · Numerical Verification — Gerofi measurement

Experimental data — Gerofi et al.

J. Gerofi, F. Deniaud, P. Friel — Interaction of Condom Design and User Techniques and Condom Acceptability

Gerofi measured the pressure exerted by two condoms on a rigid cylindrical mandrel (C = 135 mm):

WHO-Hankook condom (Hankook model, supplied by WHO)

  • Nominal width: w = 51.5 mm → C₀ = 103.0 mm
  • Stretch: λ_θ = 135/103.0 = 1.311
  • Thickness: t₀ = 0.069 mm · R = 21.49 mm
  • Measured pressure: ΔP = 2.3 kPa
ΔP = [2C₁ t₀ / R] · (λ − λ−3)
= [2C₁ × 6.9×10−5 / 0.02150] × (1.311 − 1.311−3)
= C₁ × 5.5657×10−3 × (1.311 − 0.444)
= C₁ × 5.5657×10−3
C₁ = 2300 / 5566 = 0.4132 MPa  ⟹  E = 2.48 MPa

USAID-Sultan condom (Sultan model, supplied by USAID)

  • Nominal width: w = 52.8 mm → C₀ = 105.6 mm
  • Stretch: λ_θ = 135/105.6 = 1.278
  • Thickness: t₀ = 0.061 mm · R = 21.49 mm
  • Measured pressure: ΔP = 2.0 kPa
ΔP = [2C₁ t₀ / R] · (λ − λ−3)
= [2C₁ × 6.1×10−5 / 0.02150] × (1.278 − 1.278−3)
= C₁ × 4.5413×10−3 × (1.278 − 0.479)
= C₁ × 4.5413×10−3
C₁ = 2000 / 4541 = 0.4404 MPa  ⟹  E = 2.64 MPa

Both back-calculations yield E ∈ [2.5 ; 2.6] MPa, consistent with the literature for condom latex (1.5 – 4 MPa), validating the model.
