Bibliography

Scientific and normative references used in the construction of the biomechanical model, calibration of material parameters, and validation of results.

01 Material mechanics — Neo-Hookean model
Mechanics
Rivlin, R.S. (1948)
Large elastic deformations of isotropic materials. IV. Further developments of the general theory.
Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society of London. Series A, Mathematical and Physical Sciences, 241(835), 379–397.
DOI: 10.1098/rsta.1948.0024
Theoretical foundation of the incompressible neo-Hookean model. Strain energy W = C₁(I₁ − 3), Cauchy stress tensor, incompressibility condition λ_r·λ_θ·λ_z = 1.
Mechanics
Treloar, L.R.G. (1975)
The Physics of Rubber Elasticity (3rd ed.).
Clarendon Press, Oxford. ISBN 978-0-19-857001-8.
Modelling of natural rubber as an incompressible elastic solid. Relation C₁ = E/6 for ν = 0.5 (incompressible material). Foundational reference for latex condom modelling.
02 Material parameters — Young's modulus and friction
Tribology
Cooper, B.G., Chin, S.L., Xiao, R., Buch, K., Kim, D., & Grinstaff, M.W. (2018)
Friction-lowering capabilities and human subject preferences for a hydrophilic surface coating on latex substrates: implications for increasing condom usage.
Royal Society Open Science, 5(10), 180291.
DOI: 10.1098/rsos.180291
Primary source for E = 1.69 MPa (Young's modulus of condom latex measured by uniaxial tensile testing) E = 1.69 MPa is used as the reference parameter in all CondomFit calculators. The measured friction coefficients (μ = 0.10 water-based, μ = 0.15 silicone) are documented here for information — the axial friction model σ_z = μ·σ_θ not being experimentally validated, it is not implemented in CondomFit.
03 Biomechanical measurements — Membrane and bead pressure
Biomechanics
Gerofi, J., Deniaud, F., & Friel, P. (1995)
Interaction of condom design and user techniques and condom acceptability.
Contraception, 52(4), 223–228.
DOI: 10.1016/0010-7824(95)00183-B PMID: 8605780
Source of experimental reference data: membrane pressure (film press) and bead pressure measured on two condoms (WHO-Hankook and USAID-Sultan) on a rigid cylindrical mandrel (C = 135 mm). These measurements calibrate and validate the CondomFit neo-Hookean model. Data used: Hankook w=51.5mm, t₀=0.069mm, L=192mm, d_bead=1.56mm, ΔP_film=2.3kPa, ΔP_bead=23.4kPa; Sultan w=52.8mm, t₀=0.061mm, L=184mm, d_bead=1.85mm, ΔP_film=2.0kPa, ΔP_bead=22.5kPa.
04 Standards and norms
Standard
International Organization for Standardization (2015)
ISO 4074:2015 — Natural rubber latex male condoms — Requirements and test methods.
ISO, Geneva, Switzerland.
ISO 59718
International standard defining the nominal width w = 52 mm as the reference size, burst resistance and water-tightness test methods. Normative context for the w = 52 mm choice analysed in CondomFit context pages.
05 Population anatomical data
Epidemiology
Wessells, H., Lue, T.F., & McAninch, J.W. (1996)
Penile length in the flaccid and erect states: guidelines for penile augmentation.
The Journal of Urology, 156(3), 995–997.
DOI: 10.1016/S0022-5347(01)65682-9 PMID: 8709382
Prospective measurement of erect penile length and circumference under pharmacologically induced erection in 80 men. First study to measure the penis in all three states (flaccid, stretched, erect). Contributes to the weighted cross-study mean used in CondomFit.
Epidemiology
Da Ros, C.T., Teloken, C., Sogari, P.R., Silva, F.L., Barcelos Filho, M., & Souto, C.A.V. (1993)
Estudo da dimensão peniana em ereção fármaco-induzida. [Study of penile dimensions under pharmacologically induced erection.]
Jornal Brasileiro de Urologia, 19(3), 130–132. LILACS: lil-134150.
LILACS: lil-134150
Measurement of penile length, proximal circumference (119.2 mm ± 16.5 mm) and distal circumference (110.5 mm ± 11.6 mm) under drug-induced erection in 150 men with no complaint about penile size. Brazilian study, Caucasian population. Contributes to the weighted cross-study mean.
Epidemiology
Schneider, T., Sperling, H., Lümmen, G., Syllwasschy, J., & Rübben, H. (2001)
Does penile size in younger men cause problems in condom use? A prospective measurement of penile dimensions in 111 young and 32 older men.
Urology, 57(2), 314–318.
DOI: 10.1016/S0090-4295(00)00925-0 PMID: 11182344
Prospective measurement of penile dimensions in 111 young men (18–19 years) and 32 older men (40–68 years). Erect base width: 39.5 mm (young group) and 35.0 mm (older group). Study directly motivated by the question of condom size adequacy. Contributes to the weighted cross-study mean.
CondomFit synthesis
Wessells et al. (1996), Da Ros et al. (1993), Schneider et al. (2001)
Weighted cross-study mean — erect penile circumference (n = 373).
CondomFit calculation from the three studies above (n_total = 80 + 150 + 111 + 32 = 373).
Weighted mean: C̄ = 121.7 mm, estimated SD = 14.5 mm. This validates the use of TheyFit data (C̄ = 119.9 mm, σ = 15.1 mm) as the reference in CondomFit. Although not peer-reviewed, TheyFit data benefit from a much larger sample size (n > 20,000) and illustrate a higher σ, consistent with the variability expected in a large general population. The convergence of both sources (121.7 mm vs 119.9 mm) reinforces the robustness of the central parameter used.
Proprietary data
TheyFit / myONE Perfect Fit (2013, aggregated data)
FitKit measurement database — erect penile circumference distribution (n > 20,000).
TheyFit Ltd (UK) / ONE Condoms (US). Unpublished data, reported by Joe Nelson (TheyFit founder) via public communication and blog condomsizeandfacts.blogspot.com (2013).
Source of the statistical distribution parameters used in CondomFit: C̄ = 119.9 mm, σ = 15.1 mm. Although not peer-reviewed, these data are validated by convergence with the weighted mean from clinical studies (121.7 mm), and benefit from a very large sample size (n > 20,000) illustrating real-world variability in the general population. Proprietary data — interpret with caution.

Bivariate distribution (circumference × length) simulated by Monte Carlo across 12 cohorts. Allows visualisation of an individual's volume percentile and comparison of inter-study distributions. Graphical complement to the anatomical data in section 05.
Model validation note
The CondomFit biomechanical model is calibrated on Gerofi et al. (1995) measurements by independent back-calculation: the effective Young's modulus is derived from membrane pressure measurements (E_Hankook = 2.58 MPa, E_Sultan = 2.80 MPa), while the bead modulus is derived from bead pressure measurements (E_bead_Hankook = 1.48 MPa, E_bead_Sultan = 1.33 MPa). These values differ from E = 1.69 MPa (Cooper 2018) measured by uniaxial tensile testing on unaged latex — the discrepancy is attributed to different measurement conditions (preformed condom latex vs. flat sheet) and material ageing.
